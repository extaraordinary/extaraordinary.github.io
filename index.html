Bio

I am a passionate Materials Scientist dedicated to advancing sustainable energy technologies through predictive modeling, high-performance computing, and machine learning. My research focuses on designing and understanding high-entropy oxides for thermal energy conversion devices, with an emphasis on phonons and vibrational properties. By integrating first-principles calculations and AI-driven approaches—such as Graph Neural Networks—I aim to accelerate the discovery of materials with tailored, energy-efficient properties. Building on extensive collaborations within NSF’s MRSEC and across interdisciplinary teams, I try to develop innovative computational workflows that tackle complex challenges in energy systems. Beyond research, I actively engage in K–12 and sustainability outreach initiatives and have held leadership roles to foster strong professional communities. My ultimate goal is to create atomistic-level methodologies that not only push the boundaries of materials science but also contribute to a more sustainable and equitable future.
